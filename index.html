Section 01

Brand Foundation

01

Describe your business in 2-3 sentences, as if explaining it to a stranger at dinner.

Avoid jargon. Focus on what you do, who you serve, and why it matters.

02

What problem do you solve, and what does life look like for your customer after working with you?

Think transformation: the before and after.

03

Paint a vivid picture of your ideal customer.

Age, lifestyle, values, aspirations, what they read, how they spend their weekends.

04

List 2-4 competitors or similar brands. What do they do well, and where's the gap you fill?

These don't need to be direct competitors, any brand your audience might also consider.

05

If your brand were a person, how would they walk into a room?

Presence, tone of voice, how they dress, how they make people feel. All personality, no product.

07

What are the 3-5 core values your brand will never compromise on?

The beliefs that drive decisions even when it's costly.

08

Your positioning in one line. Rough is fine, just try.

09

What does success look like for your brand 3 years from now, and what role does visual identity play in getting there?

Long-term vision inspires both your work and your audience.

Section 02

Visual Direction

10

Share 3-5 brands or visual references you admire, inside or outside your industry.

Logos, websites, packaging, editorial layouts, even spaces. Links or names both work.

11

Name 2-3 brands whose visual identity you actively dislike, and briefly say why.

Knowing what to avoid is just as valuable as knowing what to pursue.

12

Which color directions feel right for your brand? Select all that apply.

Don't overthink, gut instinct only.

13

Are there any colors that are completely off-limits?

Due to personal preference, industry association, or competitor use.

14

Which typography feel best matches your brand? Select up to two.

Fonts reflect your voice. Choose the character you want to project.

15

Which aesthetic direction best captures the visual world your brand should live in? Select up to two.

Think about texture, attitude, and energy. Focus on the overall feel, not just keywords.

16

If your brand were a physical space, what would it look and feel like inside?

Materials, lighting, atmosphere. This reveals your brand's texture beyond a screen.

17

Do you have existing brand assets that must carry into the new identity?

If starting fresh, write "none." If keeping elements, explain what and why.

19
