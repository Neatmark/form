Section 01

Brand Foundation

01

Describe your business by completing this: "I help [who] do/achieve [what] so they can [outcome]."

Avoid jargon. If you serve more than one audience, write one line per audience.

02

Before: what does your client struggle with? After: what does life look like once they've worked with you?

Write it as two short paragraphs, one for the before and one for the after. The clearer this is, the better your brand can speak to it.

03

Describe your ideal client as a real person, not just a demographic.

How do they spend their time? What do they care about? How do they want to be seen? What kind of brands do they already love?

04

What specific frustration or desire brought this client to you, rather than figuring it out on their own or going somewhere else?

Think about the moment they decided to look for help. What had changed? What were they finally ready to do?

05

List 2–4 competitors or similar brands. What do you believe they get wrong or ignore?

Answer from your own conviction, no research needed. What do you see that they're missing?

06

If your brand were a person, how would they walk into a room?

Presence, tone of voice, how they dress, how they make people feel. All personality, no product.

07

Complete this sentence: "We are the only _____ that _____.

Your positioning in one line. Don't aim for perfect. Rough is better than empty.

08

What is this brand being built for? What's the context?

A product launch in 6 weeks, a rebrand ahead of a funding round, a quiet refresh for personal reasons. This context shapes everything about how we design.
